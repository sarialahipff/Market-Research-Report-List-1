<p><ul><li>Absorbable</li><li>Nonabsorbable</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical surgical sutures market is primarily divided into two types: absorbable and nonabsorbable sutures. Absorbable sutures are designed to be broken down by the body over time, eliminating the need for removal, making them ideal for internal tissues that heal gradually. Nonabsorbable sutures, conversely, are made from materials that do not dissolve and must be removed after the tissue has healed, providing longer-lasting support for wounds needing more stability. Both types are crucial for various surgical applications.</p></p>
