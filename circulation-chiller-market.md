<p><p>The circulation chiller market primarily consists of two types: air-cooled chillers and water-cooled chillers. Air-cooled chillers utilize ambient air to dissipate heat and are typically more compact, making them suitable for smaller installations and environments with limited water supply. Conversely, water-cooled chillers rely on a water source for heat exchange, offering higher efficiency and cooling capacity, ideal for larger facilities. Both types serve various industrial and commercial applications, influencing energy consumption and operational costs.</p></p>
